Indian Army

Founded:           April 1, 1895
Headquarters:          New Delhi, India
Motto:              "Service Before Self"
Type:                                    Army
Role:                            Warfare

The Indian Army wikipedia is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces.The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Army, and its professional head is the Chief of Army Staff (COAS), who is a four-star general.

The Indian Army's primary mission is to ensure national security and national unity,defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and maintain peace and security within its borders. It conducts humanitarian rescue operations during natural calamities and other disturbances, such as
Operation Surya Hope, and can also be requisitioned by the government to cope with internal threats.

Here are some key milestones in the history of the Indian Army:

  • Dedication and Sacrifice:Our soldiers embody the true meaning of dedication and sacrifice. Leaving behind their families and the comforts of home,
    they stand vigilant at our borders, ensuring the safety and freedom of our nation. Each soldier's unwavering commitment to duty is a beacon of hope
    and inspiration for us all.

  • Courage and Valor: In the face of danger, our soldiers display unparalleled courage and valor. Whether it's the heat of battle or peacekeeping missions
    in distant lands, their bravery knows no bounds. Their fearless actions protect not just our lives but the values and principles we hold dear.

  • Community and Support: The support system within the army community, including families, veterans, and fellow soldiers, is a cornerstone of their strength. Their collective solidarity and resilience create a powerful network that sustains them through the toughest times. We owe a debt of gratitude
    to their families, whose sacrifices are equally profound.

  • Humanitarian Spirit Beyond the battlefield, our soldiers exhibit a profound humanitarian spirit. In times of natural disasters and emergencies, they are among the first to respond, providing aid and relief to those in need. Their compassion and dedication to service reflect the very best of humanity.

Battle Power:

  • Loyalty: Faithfully upholding the Constitution and serving the nation.
  • Duty: Fulfilling professional, legal, and moral obligations.
  • Respect: Treating people as they should be treated.
  • Selfless Service: Putting the welfare of the nation, the Army, and subordinates before your own.
  • Honor: Living up to the Army's values.
  • Integrity: Doing what is right, legally and morally.
  • Personal Courage: Facing fear, danger, or adversity, both physical and moral.

The Kargil War, also known as the Kargil Conflict, took place between May and July 1999 in the Kargil district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. It was a high-altitude warfare fought between India and Pakistan
By July 1999, Indian forces had successfully recaptured most of the territories infiltrated by the Pakistani troops. The war officially ended on July 26, 1999, which is now celebrated in India as Kargil Vijay Diwas (Kargil Victory Day).
The Kargil War highlighted the bravery and resilience of the Indian armed forces. It also brought international attention to the ongoing conflict between India and Pakistan over Kashmir. The war reaffirmed the strategic importance of maintaining strong defense capabilities in high-altitude regions.
